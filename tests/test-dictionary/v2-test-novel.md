# The Lantern at Moorwick Station

At dusk, Mira found the abandoned platform of Moorwick Station humming beneath a violet sky. A solitary lantern swung above the ticket window, though no wind crossed the tracks. Inside the office, she discovered a meticulous timetable covered with silver dust and a resilient brass key tied to blue thread.

The key opened a cabinet where old letters waited in a brittle stack. Each letter described a peculiar train that arrived only when someone needed courage. Mira laughed at the legend until a distant whistle rolled across the meadow. The rails began to glow, and a translucent engine slid from the fog with quiet dignity.

A conductor in a charcoal coat stepped down. He looked weary but benevolent. "One passage," he said, "to the place you most regret leaving."

Mira thought of her brother, of their final quarrel, of the apology she had hidden for years. Her voice trembled, yet she climbed aboard. The carriage smelled of rain, cedar, and warm bread. As the train moved, every window showed a different memory, some tender, some merciless.

By dawn, Mira returned to Moorwick with an envelope in her hand. She did not know whether magic had forgiven her, but she finally knew where to begin.
